Structured Interview

As the name suggests, structured interviews are very strict about how participants are able to answer the interviewer's questions.

Questions are created beforehand and typically read verbatim. The participant then might be limitted to a multiple choice answer, or, in the case of a verbal response from the participant, the interviewer might attempt to steer the participant back to the question's topic should the participant start to talk about something not directly related to the question
